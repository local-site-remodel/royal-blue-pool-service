If you're buying a new pool or replacing a failed filter, you'll face a choice between three main types: cartridge, diatomaceous earth (DE), and sand. Each has real advantages and disadvantages that affect your daily maintenance experience, water clarity, energy costs, and long-term expenses.

After servicing all three filter types across hundreds of pools in Midlothian and Ellis County, here's my honest take.

## How Each Filter Works

### Cartridge Filters
Cartridge filters use pleated polyester fabric elements — similar in principle to an air filter in your car — to trap particles as water flows through. The cartridges are housed in a sealed tank. When pressure climbs 8-10 PSI above baseline, the cartridges need to be removed and cleaned by hosing them off.

### DE (Diatomaceous Earth) Filters
DE filters use a series of grids inside a pressurized tank, coated with diatomaceous earth — a white powder made from fossilized microscopic algae. Water passes through the DE-coated grids, and the DE captures particles at the microscopic level. When the filter gets dirty, you backwash it (reverse the flow to flush debris out) and recharge with fresh DE powder.

### Sand Filters
Sand filters pump water down through a bed of filter sand and through a central lateral system that returns clean water to the pool. Particles get trapped between sand grains. When pressure rises, you backwash the filter by reversing flow through the sand to flush debris out the waste line.

## Cartridge Filters: Best For Most Residential Pools

Cartridge filters have become the dominant choice for residential pools in the Midlothian area for several reasons:

**No backwashing:** Cartridge filters don't require a backwash valve or a waste line for cleaning. This is significant in areas with water restrictions and for pool owners who want simpler operation.

**Energy efficiency:** Cartridge filters operate at lower pressure than DE or sand filters. Lower pressure means your pump works less hard, which translates to real electricity savings over the season.

**Ease of cleaning:** Pull out the cartridges, hose them off, reinstall. No DE powder to handle, no backwash procedure to follow.

**Where cartridge filters fall short:**
- The cartridges need replacement every 1-3 years (cost: $40-$200 depending on size)
- They can clog quickly in pools with heavy debris load (lots of nearby trees)
- They filter to 10-15 microns — good, but not as fine as DE

**Best for:** Most residential pools in Midlothian, pools without existing backwash lines, pool owners who want simple operation.

## DE Filters: Best Water Clarity Available

If water clarity is your top priority — particularly if you have allergy-sensitive swimmers, use the pool for competition, or simply want the clearest possible water — DE filters deliver results that cartridge and sand filters genuinely cannot match.

The 3-5 micron filtration level of DE filters is remarkable. For reference, bacteria are typically 1-10 microns. DE filtration catches particles so small they're invisible to the naked eye.

**Where DE filters fall short:**
- DE powder handling is messier than other filter types
- Backwashing requires a waste line and wastes water
- The DE grids require annual inspection and periodic replacement
- If the DE grids develop tears, DE powder passes through into the pool — a nuisance but not dangerous
- Higher initial cost

**Best for:** Pools with allergy-sensitive swimmers, pool owners who want the absolute best water clarity, competition or commercial pools.

## Sand Filters: The Old Standby

Sand filters are the most common filter type in older pools in our area — they've been around the longest and are familiar to most pool technicians. They're simple and durable.

**Advantages of sand filters:**
- Very simple operation — backwash when pressure rises, done
- Sand media lasts 5-7 years before needing replacement
- Low maintenance complexity
- Generally lower initial cost

**Where sand filters fall short:**
- Filtration at 20-40 microns is significantly coarser than cartridge or DE. In practice, this means the water isn't as polished.
- Over time, sand channels — develops channels where water flows preferentially, bypassing much of the filtering bed. This drastically reduces effectiveness without any obvious pressure change.
- Backwashing wastes significant water (50-100 gallons per backwash cycle)

**Best for:** Pools with existing sand filter setups where replacement makes sense, pools with simpler filtration needs.

## What Filter Do We Recommend?

For a new pool installation or a filter replacement in Midlothian, our standard recommendation is a correctly sized cartridge filter. The combination of good filtration, low energy consumption, no backwashing, and simple maintenance makes it the best fit for most residential pools.

If you have heavy tree debris, allergy-sensitive family members, or simply want the absolute clearest water, DE is worth the additional complexity and cost.

Sand filters make sense if you're replacing an existing sand setup where the plumbing is already configured for backwash and cost is the primary consideration.

Whatever filter type you have, the most important factor is **proper maintenance**. Any filter that's overdue for cleaning or has damaged internals is delivering less than its rated performance.
