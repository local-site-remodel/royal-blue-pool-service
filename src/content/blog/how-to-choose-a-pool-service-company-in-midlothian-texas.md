Choosing a pool service company in Midlothian is more than finding someone who shows up and puts chemicals in your water. The right company protects a significant asset (your pool is often one of the largest investments in your backyard), keeps your family safe, and provides honest assessments that save you money over time. The wrong company creates problems that cost far more to fix than the service itself.

After 20 years in this business, I know what distinguishes companies that do this right from those that don't. Here's what to look for.

## 1. Texas State Licensing (TICL)

Texas requires pool service companies to hold a license from the Texas Department of Licensing and Regulation (TDLR). The license is called a TICL — Texas Irrigator, Cleaner License — and it requires documented training, passing an exam, and ongoing education.

Before hiring any pool service company in Midlothian, ask for their TICL license number and verify it at the TDLR website (tdlr.texas.gov). An unlicensed company operating in Texas is violating state law — and if something goes wrong with your equipment or pool during their service, you may have limited recourse.

Royal Blue Pool Service holds TICL #1056.

**Red flag:** A company that can't provide a license number, gets defensive when you ask, or asks you not to worry about it.

## 2. Insurance

A pool service technician is working on your property with chemicals, equipment, and access to your home's exterior. Ask for proof of general liability insurance and verify that it's current.

Insurance protects you if:
- A technician damages equipment during service
- A chemical spill damages your deck or landscaping
- A technician is injured on your property

An uninsured company shifts all of that risk onto you.

**Red flag:** "We're insured" with no ability to provide a certificate of insurance on request.

## 3. Manufacturer Certifications

For equipment-related services, manufacturer certifications matter. Certifications like Pentair Warranty Service Center authorization and Polaris Warranty Dealer status mean the company has undergone specific training and meets the manufacturer's standards for repair and warranty work.

These certifications give you:
- Warranty repairs handled locally rather than shipping to a depot
- Access to genuine OEM parts through authorized channels
- Factory-trained technicians who know the equipment inside and out

Not every pool service company is willing to pursue and maintain these certifications — it takes training investment and ongoing compliance. Companies that do pursue them are demonstrating a commitment to quality that goes beyond the minimum.

**Red flag:** A company that claims to service Pentair equipment under warranty without being an authorized dealer.

## 4. Transparent Pricing

Quality pool service companies provide:
- Clear pricing for standard weekly service plans
- Written estimates before any repair work begins
- No surprise charges on invoices
- Upfront disclosure of parts costs vs. labor costs

Vague pricing structures — "we'll let you know what it costs after we're done" — are a setup for unexpected bills.

**Red flag:** Reluctance to provide written estimates, resistance to explaining what charges are for, or significant discrepancy between quoted and invoiced prices.

## 5. Service Documentation

Every professional pool service should leave some form of documentation after each visit:
- What was tested
- What the readings were
- What chemicals were added
- Any equipment issues observed

This documentation protects you (you know what was done), helps track trends in your pool's chemistry over time, and holds the service company accountable.

**Red flag:** No service documentation, or vague records that don't include actual chemical readings.

## 6. Honest Assessments, Not Upselling

The best pool service companies tell you what your pool actually needs — not what generates the most revenue. This means:
- Not recommending replacement when repair is appropriate
- Not adding unnecessary chemicals or treatments
- Not recommending services you don't need
- Giving you honest timelines for equipment replacement

This is admittedly hard to evaluate before you've worked with a company. But you can get signals from initial interactions: Are they focused on understanding your pool, or on closing a service contract? Do they explain their recommendations, or just tell you what to buy?

**Red flag:** A technician who recommends a laundry list of services and products on the first visit without taking time to understand your pool's history.

## 7. Local Presence and Reputation

A company that's been serving Midlothian and Ellis County for years has a track record you can research. Check Google reviews, Yelp reviews, Nextdoor recommendations, and Alignable ratings. Look for patterns — not just the rating, but what customers say about reliability, honesty, and quality.

Local companies have a stake in their community reputation in a way that larger franchises sometimes don't. When Derrick and Nicole Jackson service your pool, their personal and business reputation is attached to every visit.

**Red flag:** No local online presence, no reviews from Midlothian-area customers, or reviews that mention repeated reliability problems.

## Questions to Ask Before Hiring

When interviewing pool service companies, ask:

1. What is your TICL license number?
2. Are you licensed and insured? Can I get a certificate?
3. Are you a Certified Pentair or Polaris warranty dealer?
4. What does your weekly service include and what does it cost?
5. Will I receive documentation after each service visit?
6. How do you handle warranty repairs on equipment?
7. What is your process for recommending repairs or replacements?
8. How long have you been serving the Midlothian area?

A quality company will answer all of these questions directly and without hesitation. Defensiveness or vague answers to any of these questions is a signal to keep looking.
