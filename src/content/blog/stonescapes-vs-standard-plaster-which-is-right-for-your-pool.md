If you're planning a pool replaster or remodel, you'll eventually face the same question every pool owner asks: is Stonescapes worth the extra money, or should I go with standard white plaster?

Having applied both finishes across hundreds of pools in Midlothian and Ellis County over the past 20 years, I can give you an honest answer based on real-world performance — not a sales pitch.

## What is Standard White Plaster?

Standard white plaster (also called "white plaster" or "marcite") is a blend of white Portland cement, white marble dust, and water. It's been the standard pool finish for decades, and it has some genuine advantages:

- **Low upfront cost** — typically $3,000-$5,000 for an average residential pool
- **Smooth surface** — comfortable underfoot when new
- **Classic look** — bright white finish makes pool water appear a distinctive bright blue
- **Widely available** — most pool contractors apply it

The problems with standard plaster are largely about longevity. In the Texas climate, standard white plaster typically lasts **5-7 years** before showing significant wear:

- Yellowing and staining from iron in fill water
- Roughness from acid washing and chemical etching
- Chalkiness from calcium leaching
- Pitting and scaling in hard-water areas like ours

When standard plaster deteriorates, the rough surface harbors bacteria and algae, feels uncomfortable on feet and skin, and is essentially impossible to restore chemically — you need to replaster.

## What is Stonescapes?

Stonescapes is a brand of pebble aggregate pool finish manufactured by NPP (Natural Pebble Products). Instead of smooth white cement, it blends natural river pebbles with white Portland cement to create a textured, durable surface.

The pebble aggregate approach has several structural advantages:

1. **Thickness and density** — Pebble finishes are applied thicker than standard plaster, making them more resistant to chipping and impact damage
2. **Pore resistance** — The pebbles create a tighter surface matrix that resists stain penetration
3. **Chemical resistance** — Less susceptible to acid etching from low-pH chemistry

Stonescapes comes in numerous color blends — Tahoe Blue, Island White, Aqua Blue, Java, Caribbean Blue, and more — each creating a distinctly different water appearance.

## A Real-World Durability Comparison

| Factor | Standard Plaster | Stonescapes |
|---|---|---|
| Expected lifespan | 5-7 years | 15-20 years |
| Stain resistance | Low | High |
| Roughness over time | Becomes rough in 3-5 years | Maintains surface integrity |
| Color fading | Yellows and stains | Color blends age well |
| Acid wash frequency | Every 2-4 years | Rarely needed |
| Comfort underfoot | Smooth when new, rough after 3-5 years | Pebble texture throughout life |

The lifespan difference is significant. If you replaster with standard plaster at $4,500 and it lasts 6 years, you're paying $750/year for your finish. If you replaster with Stonescapes at $6,500 and it lasts 18 years, you're paying $361/year — roughly half the annual cost.

## Water Appearance: How Each Finish Affects Color

The finish you choose dramatically affects how your pool water looks:

**Standard white plaster:** Creates a bright, almost shocking blue-white appearance. Beautiful when the plaster is new. As it yellows and stains, the water can take on an unappealing greenish or grey cast.

**Stonescapes color blends:**
- *Tahoe Blue* — Creates deep sapphire-blue water with a natural stone texture. Extremely popular in our market.
- *Island White* — Lighter pebble blend creating a turquoise Caribbean appearance
- *Aqua Blue* — Medium-depth teal appearance
- *Caribbean Blue* — Bold tropical blue with green undertones
- *Java* — Darker pebble blend creating a deep, sophisticated lagoon appearance

Many homeowners choose their Stonescapes color based on the appearance they want in the water rather than the finish color itself. We're happy to show you real photos of completed jobs in different blends.

## What About Wet Edge Primera Stone?

Wet Edge Primera Stone is another premium finish option we apply. It's a quartz aggregate finish — finer than Stonescapes pebble, using crushed marble and colored quartz rather than river rock. The result is a smoother texture than Stonescapes with similar durability advantages.

Wet Edge is an excellent choice for homeowners who:
- Want premium durability but prefer a smoother texture than pebble
- Want a specific color that's available in the Wet Edge palette
- Have a spa where the smooth texture is preferred

Wet Edge lifespan is typically 12-15 years — between standard plaster and premium Stonescapes.

## The Honest Recommendation

For most pools in the Midlothian area, we recommend Stonescapes if you plan to stay in your home for more than 7-10 years. The long-term economics are compelling, the water appearance is superior, and the surface holds up better against our Texas water chemistry and heat.

Standard plaster makes sense if:
- You're on a tight budget right now and just need the pool functional
- You're planning to sell your home in the next few years and want minimum investment
- You specifically prefer the traditional bright white look
