If you're a pool owner in Midlothian, DeSoto, or anywhere across Ellis County, you've probably asked this question: how often does my pool actually need professional service? The answer depends on several factors — but for most Texas homeowners, the answer is weekly, and here's why.

## Why Texas Pools Need More Frequent Service

Texas summers are brutal. Water temperatures regularly climb above 85°F, which dramatically accelerates chemical consumption and algae growth. A pool that might need service every two weeks in a cooler climate needs attention every week in our climate. Here's what's happening to your pool when temperatures spike:

- **Chlorine burns off faster** — UV radiation and heat destroy free chlorine at roughly twice the rate seen in northern states
- **Algae thrives** — Green algae can colonize a pool within 24-48 hours of chlorine dropping below the safe threshold
- **Evaporation is significant** — Ellis County pools can lose 1-2 inches of water per week in July and August, affecting skimmer function
- **Calcium hardness shifts** — Evaporation concentrates minerals, causing scale buildup on tile and equipment

## The Weekly Service Standard

For most residential pools in the Midlothian area, weekly professional service is the minimum recommended frequency during the swim season (April through October). Here's what a proper weekly visit covers:

### Water Chemistry Testing and Balancing
Every visit should include testing of:
- **Free chlorine** (ideal range: 1-3 ppm)
- **pH** (ideal range: 7.4-7.6)
- **Total alkalinity** (ideal range: 80-120 ppm)
- **Cyanuric acid / stabilizer** (ideal range: 30-50 ppm)
- **Calcium hardness** (ideal range: 200-400 ppm)

Imbalanced chemistry doesn't just make the water uncomfortable — it actively damages your pool's plaster surface, corrodes metal fittings, and shortens equipment lifespan.

### Physical Cleaning Tasks
Beyond chemistry, every weekly visit should include:
- Skimming the water surface to remove leaves, insects, and debris
- Vacuuming the pool floor to remove settled dirt
- Brushing walls, steps, and the waterline tile to prevent algae attachment
- Emptying skimmer baskets and the pump strainer basket

### Equipment Inspection
A trained technician should inspect your pump, filter, heater, and automatic cleaner during every visit. This is where professional service pays for itself — small issues caught early (a worn pump seal, a slightly elevated filter pressure reading) prevent the $500-$2,000 repair bills that result from running equipment until it fails.

## What About Fall and Winter in Texas?

Here's a mistake many Texas pool owners make: cutting back service frequency in fall and winter. Our winters are mild enough that algae can still grow, particularly during the warmer stretches we see throughout November and March. We recommend maintaining weekly or at minimum bi-weekly service through the off-season for these reasons:

1. **Freeze protection** — Properly balanced water is less likely to cause equipment damage in a freeze event. If your water chemistry is off and temperatures drop to 28°F, you can experience cracked pipes and pump damage.
2. **Algae prevention** — Green algae can bloom in water temperatures as low as 50°F. It happens slowly in winter, but a neglected pool can turn green by February.
3. **Equipment monitoring** — Winter is when condensation, debris, and infrequent use tend to cause equipment problems that go unnoticed.

## Signs You Need More Frequent Service

If you're on a bi-weekly or monthly schedule and experiencing any of the following, you need to increase service frequency:

- Water is frequently cloudy or hazy between visits
- You're seeing algae growth on walls or steps before the next scheduled visit
- Your pool technician is spending most of the visit on corrective treatments rather than maintenance
- Chemical usage is much higher than expected
- Equipment problems are being discovered late (after the damage is done)

## The True Cost Comparison

Many pool owners resist weekly service because of the cost. But consider the alternative:

- A green pool recovery treatment costs $150-$400 depending on severity — equivalent to 2-3 months of weekly service
- A pool pump replacement due to running dry or overheating: $400-$1,200
- Acid wash due to neglected staining: $350-$600
- Replaster triggered prematurely by chronic chemical imbalance: $4,000-$8,000

Weekly professional maintenance is almost always less expensive than the reactive repairs that result from cutting corners.
