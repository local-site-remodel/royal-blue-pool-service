        - Typically cleared within 3-5 days

        - Appears as dark spots embedded in pool surfaces
        - Requires aggressive brushing with specialized tools
        - May take 5-7 days or longer to fully eliminate
        - Protective outer layer resists normal chlorine levels
        - Often reappears if roots are not completely removed

faqs:
  - question: How long does green pool recovery take?
    answer: Most green pools are restored to crystal-clear condition within 3 to 5 days, depending on the severity of the algae bloom. Heavily neglected pools with black algae may take up to 7 days. We monitor progress daily and adjust treatment as needed.
  - question: Can I swim in my pool during treatment?
    answer: No. During the shock treatment and recovery process, chlorine levels are elevated well beyond safe swimming ranges. We'll let you know exactly when it's safe to swim again — typically once chemical levels have returned to normal and the water tests clear.
  - question: How can I prevent my pool from turning green again?
    answer: Consistent weekly maintenance is the best prevention. This includes proper chemical balancing, regular brushing, adequate filtration run times, and maintaining correct chlorine levels. Our weekly maintenance plans are designed to prevent algae before it starts.
  - question: Why did my pool turn green even though I add chlorine?
    answer: A green pool despite adding chlorine usually means your stabilizer (cyanuric acid) levels are too high, your filter isn't running long enough, or your pH is out of range — all of which reduce chlorine's effectiveness. A professional water test can identify the root cause quickly.
