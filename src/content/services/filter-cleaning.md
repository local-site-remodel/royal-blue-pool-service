faqs:
  - question: How often should pool filters be cleaned?
    answer: Most pool filters in the Midlothian area should be cleaned every 3 to 6 months, depending on usage, surrounding vegetation, and filter type. Cartridge filters typically need cleaning more frequently than DE or sand filters. We recommend checking your filter pressure gauge weekly — when it reads 8-10 PSI above the clean starting pressure, it is time for a cleaning.
  - question: What is the difference between cartridge and DE filters?
    answer: Cartridge filters use a pleated polyester element to trap particles down to 10-15 microns and are cleaned by hosing off the cartridge. DE (diatomaceous earth) filters use grids coated with DE powder to filter particles as small as 3-5 microns, providing the finest filtration available. DE filters require backwashing and recharging with fresh DE powder. Both types are common in Midlothian pools.
  - question: How do I know if my pool filter needs to be replaced?
    answer: Signs that your filter element needs replacement include the filter pressure stays high even after cleaning, visible tears or fraying in the cartridge fabric, cracked or collapsed DE grids, persistent cloudy water despite proper chemical balance, and filter elements that are more than 2-3 years old for cartridges or 5-7 years old for DE grids. We inspect every filter during service and advise on replacement timing.
  - question: Can a dirty filter damage my pool pump?
    answer: Yes. A clogged or dirty filter forces your pump to work harder, increasing pressure and energy consumption. Over time this added strain can overheat the pump motor, damage seals, and significantly shorten the pump lifespan. Regular filter maintenance protects your entire pool circulation system and can save you hundreds of dollars in equipment repairs.
