faqs:
  - question: How long does a pool replaster take?
    answer: Most replaster jobs take 5 to 7 days from drain to fill. We drain the pool, prep the surface by chipping and bond-coating, apply the new finish, then do a controlled fill and chemical balance. Weather and pool size can shift the timeline slightly, but we keep you informed at every stage.
  - question: What is the difference between Stonescapes and standard white plaster?
    answer: Standard white plaster is a smooth cement finish that typically lasts 5 to 7 years before showing stains or roughness. Stonescapes is a pebble-aggregate finish by NPP that blends natural stone with white cement for a textured, durable surface that resists staining and lasts 15 to 20 years. It also comes in multiple color blends to match your backyard design.
  - question: Can I upgrade my tile during a replaster?
    answer: Absolutely. A replaster is the ideal time to replace waterline tile, add glass tile accents, or upgrade coping. Since the pool is already drained and prepped, combining these projects saves time and reduces overall cost compared to doing them separately.
  - question: How much does pool remodeling cost in Midlothian?
    answer: Costs vary based on pool size, finish selection, and scope of work. A basic replaster starts around $4,000 to $6,000 for an average-sized residential pool, while premium Stonescapes or Wet Edge finishes and tile upgrades range higher. We provide free on-site estimates so you get an accurate quote with no surprises.
